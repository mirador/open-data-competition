The Mirador Open Data Competition

About

Mirador is a software tool to explore complex datasets and help you find new correlations and patterns in the data. We believe that open acess to scientific, health, and goverment data (among many other areas) promotes public participation and transparency in research and governance. So, we put together the Mirador Open Data Competition to highlight the importance of Open Data and also to give you some rewards for your findings with Mirador!

We packaged four publicly available datasets in health, sports and global development, and you will have one month to explore these datasets with Mirador and submit your findings. A jury of specialists will evaluate the submissions and make a selection of seven winning entries based on novelty, significance, and potential impact.

Keep reading on to learn about the details of the competition, and how to use Mirador to explore the datasets and submit your discoveries. Thank you for your interest, and good luck!

Registration and timeline

The Mirador Open Data Competition will be open between Sunday September 28th and Tuesday October 28th. During that time, you will be able to upload your correlation discoveries from Mirador into your competition account. Create this account by clicking on the sign up button on the top of this page. After the competition period is over, the jury will select the winners after a few weeks.

Datasets

We choose four datasets in health, sports and global development that are, in our view, good representatives of Open Data initatives, by being all publicly available, well documented, and up-to-date. These datasets are also complex and rich in interesting and unexpected correlations between thousands of variables!

Jury

The jury for the Mirador Open Data Competition is consituted by experts in the respective fields each dataset comes from.

In order to succesfully participate of this competition, you need to use Mirador on any (or all) of the datasets descrived above.
