The Mirador Open Data Competition

About

Mirador is a software tool to explore complex datasets and to help you find new correlations in the data. We believe that open acess to scientific, health, and goverment data (among many other areas) promotes public participation and transparency in research and governance. So, we put together the Mirador Open Data Competition to highlight the importance of Open Data and also to give you some rewards for your findings with Mirador!

We packaged four publicly available datasets in health, sports and global development, and you will have one month to explore these datasets with Mirador and submit your findings. A jury of specialists will evaluate the submissions and make a selection of three winning entries based on novelty, significance, and potential impact.

Keep reading on to learn about the details of the competition, and how to use Mirador to explore the datasets and submit your discoveries. Thank you for your interest, and good luck!

Registration and timeline

The Mirador Open Data Competition will be open between Sunday September 28th and Tuesday October 28th. During that time, you will be able to upload your correlation discoveries from Mirador into your competition account. Create the account by clicking on the sign up button on the top of this page, then download the latest version of Mirador (either for Windows or macOSX) to get started, and grab any of the datasets described in the following section.

After the competition period is over, the jury will select the winners which we will announce in the following weeks.

Datasets

We choose four datasets in health, sports and global development that are, in our view, good representatives of Open Data initatives, by virtue of being all publicly available, well documented, and up-to-date. These datasets are also complex and rich in interesting and unexpected correlations between thousands of variables!

Prizes

There is a total of 3 cash prizes:

  2. One second prize of $200
  3. One third prize of $100

Jury

The jury for the Mirador Open Data Competition is consituted by experts in the respective fields each dataset comes from.

Instructions

In order to succesfully participate in this competition, you first need to create an account, and then download the latest version of Mirador for Mac or Windows and any of the datasets listed above. You can sumbit any number of correlations from Mirador into your competition account, but by the end of the competition period you need to select up to 10 findings for evaluation by the jury. The following videos guide you through the process, from registering your competition account, downloading the datasets, using Mirador to explore the data, and finally submitting your findings:

Getting help

Mirador is an ongoing open-source project, so if you find bugs or any other issues, please report them here, and we will try to fix them during the course of the competition. Issues concerning the account page should be reported here.

Visualizing the competition!

Finally, there is a little art project taking place at the same time as the competition... Every time you submit a new finding with Mirador, the sequence of steps from the moment you launched Mirador until you upload the correlation is also stored in your competition account. After the competition is finished, we would like to create a visualization of all these sequences, with the hope of making a beautiful representation of the process of data exploration with Mirador!
